Prompt

Design a mobile app called SONA. The app helps parents and caregivers understand where and why a person — especially a baby — is in pain, by reading signals from a paired wearable sensor and showing them as a visual body map.

DESIGN DIRECTION
Simple, modern, and minimal. Focus on clarity over decoration. The interface should feel calm and reassuring — not clinical or alarming. Let the body map be the hero of every screen. Explore your own visual direction — typography, spacing, and layout choices are up to you.

SCREENS TO DESIGN

Screen 1 — Body Map (Home)
The primary screen users see every time they open the app
A 3D anatomical body model centered on the screen
Pain signals displayed as a heatmap overlaid on the body — intensity communicates severity
A 2D / 3D toggle in the top corner
When a signal is active, a card slides up from the bottom showing: pain type in plain language, confidence level as a percentage, and 2-3 suggested actions
Bottom navigation bar with Home, History, Alerts, Settings

Screen 2 — Semantic Zoom Detail
User taps a highlighted region on the body map
View zooms into that specific body area showing more detail
Pain location becomes more precise — from general region to specific muscle or joint
Plain language label identifies the specific area
Back button returns to full body view

Screen 3 — Signal History
A scrollable timeline of past pain episodes
Each entry shows: date and time, body location, pain type, and whether it was resolved
Simple visual pattern indicator — is this signal recurring?
Export option for sharing with a doctor

Screen 4 — Escalation Alert
Full screen alert for when signals reach a critical level
Clear, calm headline explaining what was detected
Recommended next action — call doctor or go to emergency room
One large button for emergency call
Secondary option to dismiss if caregiver has already handled it

Screen 5 — Onboarding Device Pairing
Simple step-by-step screen to pair the SONA wearable sensor via Bluetooth
Visual of the wearable device
Clear progress indicator showing pairing steps
Confirmation screen once paired successfully

INTERACTION NOTES
Body map should feel alive and responsive — not static
Transitions between zoom levels should feel smooth and natural
The interpretation card should slide up gently — not pop or flash
Notifications and alerts should feel calm and informative, never panicked
Every screen should have generous spacing — never feel crowded

TONE
SONA should feel like a calm, knowledgeable friend — not a doctor, not an alarm system. Reassuring. Clear. Human.
